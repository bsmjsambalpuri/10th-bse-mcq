Chapter SLE06: A Letter to God - Activity Answers

E. Let’s understand the text

  1. Where did Lencho live?
    Lencho lived in a house located on the crest of a low hill, which was the only one in the entire valley.
  2. What did he hope for?
    He hoped for a downpour or at least a shower for his fields of ripe corn.
  3. What did he say about the raindrops?
    He said they were not just raindrops but new coins; the big drops were ten cent pieces and the little ones were fives.
  4. How did the rain change?
    A strong wind began to blow, and along with the rain, very large hailstones began to fall.
  5. What happened to Lencho’s corn fields?
    The corn was totally destroyed. The field looked white as if covered with salt, and the flowers were gone from the plants.
  6. Who did Lencho have faith in?
    Lencho had complete faith in God.
  7. Who did he write a letter to?
    He wrote a letter to God asking for a hundred pesos.
  8. Who read the letter?
    Initially, a postman saw the address and showed it to his boss, the postmaster, who then read the letter.
  9. What did the postmaster do?
    He decided to answer the letter to keep the writer's faith intact. He collected money from his employees, gave part of his salary, and sent seventy pesos to Lencho.
  10. Was Lencho surprised to find a letter for him with money in it?
    No, he showed not the slightest surprise because his confidence in God's help was very strong.
  11. What was Lencho’s reaction after getting the letter?
    He became angry when he counted the money because there were only seventy pesos instead of the hundred he had requested.

F. Let’s read between the lines

(a) Questions

  • (i) Why did Lencho keep on looking at the sky throughout the morning?
    He was waiting for the rain that his ripe corn field desperately needed.
  • (ii) Why was the field white after the storm?
    It was white because it was covered in hailstones, which resembled salt.
  • (iii) Why did Lencho say the raindrops were like ‘new coins’?
    The rain promised a good harvest, which would bring prosperity and money to his family.
  • (iv) Why did Lencho prefer locusts to the storm?
    He believed a plague of locusts would have left something behind, whereas the hailstorm left nothing at all.
  • (v) Did Lencho try to find out who had sent the money to him? Why / Why not?
    No, he did not, because he was absolutely certain that God had received his letter and sent the help.
  • (vi) What would be the reaction of the post office employees when they read the second letter?
    They would likely feel hurt and unappreciated, as they had made a charitable sacrifice only to be called a "bunch of crooks" by the person they helped.

(b) Passage Questions

  1. Who does Lencho have complete faith in?
    He has complete faith in God.
  2. ‘Lencho was an ox of a man’ – what does the line mean?
    It means he was a very strong and hardworking man who worked like an animal in the fields.
  3. What was the postmaster like?
    He was a fat, amiable, and sensitive person with a generous heart.
  4. Why did the postmaster send money to Lencho?
    He wanted to preserve Lencho’s unwavering faith in God.
  5. What does the expression ‘an act of charity’ mean?
    It refers to the selfless act of giving money or help to someone in need.

G. Let’s learn some words

(i) Matching Storms

  1. A violent tropical storm in which strong winds move in a circle: Cyclone
  2. An extremely strong wind: Gale
  3. A violent tropical storm with very strong winds: Typhoon
  4. A violent storm with strong winds (Western Atlantic): Hurricane
  5. A violent storm whose centre is a funnel-shaped cloud: Tornado
  6. A very strong wind that moves fast in a spinning movement: Whirlwind

I. Let’s learn language

(i) Relative Clauses

  1. My mother, who cooks very well, is going to host a T.V. show on cooking.
  2. Our institution, which works for public welfare, is highly popular.
  3. Satish, who was fortunate, scored a goal in the last minute.
  4. Mother Teresa, who served mankind, is revered as a saint.
  5. I often go to Mumbai, which is the commercial capital of India.
  6. These sports-persons, whose performance has been excellent, are going to meet the President.

(ii) Negatives for Emphasis

  1. The trees lost all their leaves: Not a leaf remained on the trees.
  2. The letter was addressed to God himself: It was nothing less than a letter to God.
  3. The postman saw this address for the first time in his life: Never in his career as a postman had he known that address.
