Chapter SLE11: A Tiger in the House - Activity Answers

B. Text I: Comprehension Check

  1. Who was Timothy and where was it discovered?
    Timothy was a tiger cub discovered by Grandfather in the Terai Jungle near Dehra .
  2. Why did Grandfather accompany the party?
    He was requested to accompany the hunting party because he knew the forests of the Siwalik hills better than most and could advise on the land and directions for the beaters .
  3. The writer says that the camp was very splendid. Why?
    The camp was sumptuous, featuring seven large tents, a dining tent, numerous servants' tents, and fine dining with hot-water plates and seven or eight courses in the jungle .
  4. Who discovered Timothy and when?
    Grandfather discovered Timothy during the end of the expedition, while strolling down a forest path at some distance from the rest of the party .
  5. Do you think Grandfather was very bold? Why do you think so?
    Yes, Grandfather was bold because he walked alone in the forest and picked up a tiger cub to bring home .
  6. What kind of special quality did Grandfather have? How was Grandfather different from other members of the party?
    He had an intimate knowledge of the local forests and was the only member of the expedition to successfully "bag" any game, dead or alive .
  7. Do you think Grandfather will pet the tiger cub at home?
    Yes, the text confirms he picked the cub up and brought him home after the camp broke up .

II. Text II: Comprehension Check

  1. Who are there in this part of the story?
    The characters include Timothy the tiger, Grandmother, Mahmoud the cook, Toto the monkey, a mongrel puppy, and the writer .
  2. Who named the tiger cub and who brought it up?
    Grandmother named the cub Timothy, and he was brought up initially on milk provided by Mahmoud the cook .
  3. What was the diet of the tiger cub?
    His diet started with milk, transitioned to raw mutton and cod liver oil, and eventually included pigeons and rabbits .
  4. Who were Timothy’s companions at home?
    His companions were Toto the monkey and a small mongrel puppy .
  5. What was Timothy’s favorite amusement? How did the writer become Timothy’s favourite?
    His favorite amusement was stalking anyone who played with him. The writer became a favorite because he played with Timothy after coming to live with Grandfather .
  6. What kind of change came over Timothy, when he was six months old?
    He became steadily less friendly, started stalking other pets, and began causing "frenzied cackling" in the poultry house at night .
  7. Grandfather decided to transfer Timothy to a zoo. Why?
    The decision was made when Timothy began to stalk the cook, Mahmoud, with what appeared to be villainous intent .
  8. Where did Grandfather take him and how?
    He took him to a zoo in Lucknow, two hundred miles away, traveling in a reserved first-class train compartment .

III. Text III: Comprehension Check

  1. When did Grandfather visit the zoo and why?
    He visited about six months later while in Lucknow to see how Timothy was getting on .
  2. What did Grandfather do at the cage where Timothy was kept?
    He put his arms through the bars, stroked the tiger's forehead, ticked his ears, and smacked him across the mouth whenever he growled .
  3. How did the tiger behave towards Grandfather?
    The tiger approached the bars, allowed himself to be petted, and licked Grandfather's hands with "increasing relish" .
  4. What did he suggest to the Keeper?
    He suggested moving the tiger to a different cage to get him away from a snarling leopard that was frightening him .
  5. Was the tiger really Timothy? How do you know this?
    No, it was not Timothy. The keeper revealed that Timothy had died of pneumonia two months earlier .
  6. What did the Keeper say to Grandfather about the tiger?
    He explained that Timothy was dead and that the current tiger was a dangerous animal trapped in the hills only the previous month .
  7. What made Grandfather give a scornful look to the keeper?
    He gave the look as he walked out, possibly out of grief and frustration that the dangerous situation had not been clearly communicated earlier .

D. Writing: 50-Word Answers

  1. Who was Timothy? What made Grandfather to bring him home with him?
    Timothy was a tiger cub discovered in the Terai Jungle. Grandfather brought him home because he found the eighteen-inch cub hiding among banyan roots and, as the only member of his party to bag any game, he decided to keep and pet the animal .
  2. Who were Timothy’s companions and how was he brought up at home?
    Timothy's home companions were Toto the monkey and a mongrel puppy. He was raised on a rich diet that included milk from a feeding bottle, raw mutton, cod liver oil, and eventually small animals like pigeons and rabbits .
  3. Why did Grandfather decide to transfer Timothy to a zoo?
    Grandfather decided on the transfer because Timothy grew less friendly as he aged. He began hunting poultry at night and eventually showed dangerous behavior by stalking the cook, Mahmoud, indicating he was becoming too wild for a household .
  4. What things did Grandfather do at the cage when he visited the zoo?
    During his visit, Grandfather climbed the railing, reached through the cage bars, and affectionately petted the tiger. He stroked its forehead, ticked its ears, and used his "old way" of smacking it across the mouth to keep it quiet .
