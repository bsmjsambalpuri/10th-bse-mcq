Q: Which are the lines in the poem where the poet expresses his gratitude to God?
A:
"The Lord God made them all."

"He gave us eyes to see them,
And lips that we might tell
How great is God Almighty,
Who has made all things well."

Q: Is this poem a description or a prayer?
A: This poem is primarily a hymn of praise, which makes it a form of prayer. While it contains vivid descriptions of natural beauty, its core purpose is to acknowledge the Lord God as the creator and to praise Him for making all things well.

Q: Why do you think so?
A: The poet, Cecil Frances Alexander, was a famous hymn writer. The final stanza praises God Almighty and expresses gratitude for His creation, showing that the poem is intended as a prayer and song of worship.

Trees and Their Importance

Q: Write a few lines on each clue.
A:

Tree:
Trees are beautiful creations of nature. They provide shade, oxygen, fruits, and shelter to many living beings.

Construction of a House:
Wood from trees is used for building houses, doors, windows, and roofs.

Furniture:
Trees provide timber which is used to make chairs, tables, beds, and other furniture.

Rainfall:
Trees help maintain the water cycle and play an important role in bringing rainfall.

Temperature Control:
Trees keep the environment cool and help reduce global warming.

Soil Erosion:
The roots of trees hold the soil firmly and prevent soil erosion.

Deforestation:
Deforestation means cutting down forests. It harms wildlife and disturbs the balance of nature.

Afforestation:
Afforestation means planting more trees. It helps protect the environment and ensures a greener future.
