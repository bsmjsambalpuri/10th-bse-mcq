Chapter 7: Noun Clauses and Relative Clauses

Complex sentences are composed of an independent Main Clause (M.C.) linked with one or more dependent Subordinate Clauses (S.C.).

1. Subordinate Noun Clauses

A subordinate clause is classified as a Noun Clause when it functions exactly like a noun structure within the sentence architecture.

Core Syntactic Functions:

  • Subject of the sentence: What he says is correct.
  • Object of a transitive verb: Radha said that she was happy.
  • Complement of an incomplete verb: The problem is that we do not have money.
  • Object of a preposition: Pay attention to what the teacher says.

2. Subordinate Relative Clauses

A Relative (Adjective) Clause qualifies an antecedent noun phrase in the main clause. These clauses are introduced using relative pronouns: who, whom, which, that, whose, where.

Defining vs Non-Defining Relative Clauses:

  • Defining Relative Clause: Essential to identify the noun phrase. No commas used. (e.g., "He’s the man who stole my purse").
  • Non-Defining Relative Clause: Adds extra, non-essential details about a known noun phrase. Parenthesized with commas. (e.g., "My grandmother, who was an extraordinary woman, lived to the age of one hundred").
