Chapter 10: Direct and Indirect Speech

When reporting statements made by a speaker, we can use Direct Speech (quoting exact words inside quotation marks) or Indirect/Reported Speech (modifying pronouns, tenses, and time markers to fit the reporter's context).

1. Crucial Backshifting Rules for Tense

If the introductory reporting verb is in the Past Tense, the verbs within the quoted speech backshift as follows:

  • Present Simple → Past Simple (e.g., write → wrote)
  • Present Progressive → Past Progressive (e.g., is writing → was writing)
  • Present Perfect / Past Simple → Past Perfect (e.g., has written / wrote → had written)
  • Modals: will → would | can → could | may → might

2. Adverbial Conversions (Time and Place Shift)

  • here → there
  • this → that
  • now → then
  • tomorrow → the next day / following day
  • yesterday → the day before / previous day

3. Conversions across Sentence Types

A. Interrogative (Questions)

Reporting questions use verbs like asked, enquired, or wanted to know. Sentence order flips from question format back to declarative format, removing the question mark.

Wh-Question: "Where do you live?" she asked. → She wanted to know where I lived.
Yes/No Question: "Are you leaving today?" she asked. → She asked if/whether I was leaving that day.

B. Imperative (Commands / Requests)

Uses reporting verbs like ordered, advised, requested, or told followed by a to-infinitive structure.

Direct: "Consult a doctor immediately," he said to me.
Indirect: He advised me to consult a doctor immediately.
