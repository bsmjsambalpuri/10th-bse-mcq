Chapter 3: Subject-Verb Agreement

A finite verb must agree cleanly with its subject in number and person. Singular subjects take singular finite verbs, and plural subjects take plural finite verbs.

Essential Concord Rules

Rule 1: Collective Nouns as Single Units

When a collection of nouns refers to an entire group or an absolute single quantity, the associated verb form remains singular.

  • Correct: A whole regiment of volunteers is working here.
  • Correct: The number of scholarships has increased this year.
Rule 2: Correlatives & Distributives

Words like either of, neither of, each of, or one of followed by plural nouns structurally require a singular verb format.

  • Correct: Either of the boys has won a medal.
  • Correct: One of his friends is able to speak English fluently.
Rule 3: Quantities, Time, and Distance

Plural-form phrases signifying unique time intervals, absolute sums of cash, or set units of distance take singular verbs.

  • Correct: Six weeks is a long vacation for students.
  • Correct: Three-fourths of the crop was damaged by the flood.
Rule 4: Nouns Joined with Parenthetical Expressions

When two subjects are linked using phrases like as well as, along with, or together with, the main verb matches the first subject.

  • Correct: Ram as well as his friends likes to play football.
