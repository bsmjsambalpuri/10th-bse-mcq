Chapter 9: The Passive

Passive voice transformations focus the clause on the recipient of the action (the object) rather than the performer (the agent). The agent can often be omitted if they are unknown or obvious.

Core Structural Equation

Passive Structure = Object + [Be Auxiliary matching Tense] + Past Participle (v-en)

Tense Conversion Grid

Tense Aspect Active Structural Format Passive Structural Format
Present Simple Someone steals my purse. My purse is stolen.
Past Simple Someone stole my purse yesterday. My purse was stolen yesterday.
Present Progressive The teacher is working out the sum. The sum is being worked out.
Past Progressive They were playing football. Football was being played.
Present Perfect The postman has delivered letters. Letters have been delivered.

Special Passive Patterns

  • Verbs of Reporting: Active: "People say that elephants have good memories." → Passive: "It is said that elephants have good memories" OR "Elephants are said to have good memories."
  • Imperative Sentences: Active: "Shut the door." → Passive: "Let the door be shut."
