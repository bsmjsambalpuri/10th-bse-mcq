All Things Bright and Beautiful

Chapter: All Things Bright and Beautiful

Poet: C.F. Alexander (Cecil Frances Alexander)

Story

The poem is a vivid celebration of the natural world and all its elements, ranging from the "creatures great and small" to the "things wise and wonderful". The poet describes specific details of nature, such as the glowing colours of opening flowers and the tiny wings of singing birds.

It paints a picture of a magnificent landscape featuring purple-headed mountains, a running river, and the sunset and morning that brighten the sky. The poem also mentions the cycle of nature, including the cold winter wind, the pleasant summer sun, and ripe fruit in the garden.

The poem highlights how humans interact with nature through meadows for playing and rushes by the water that people gather daily. Ultimately, it explains that God gave humans eyes and lips specifically to see and tell of His greatness.

Understanding

  • What the Poet Thinks: The poet believes that every part of the universe is a purposeful creation made "well" by the Lord God. She views even the smallest aspects of nature, like a bird's song or a flower's hue, as a reason for admiration.
  • Theme: The central theme is the praise and glory of God as the Almighty creator of all things bright, beautiful, and wonderful.
  • Insights: The chapter provides the insight that humans are blessed with the senses of sight, smell, and feeling specifically so they can enjoy and appreciate the beauty of creation. It functions as a hymn of gratitude, teaching that acknowledging the beauty in the world is a way to recognize the wisdom of the creator.
