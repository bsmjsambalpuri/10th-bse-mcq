Chapter 8: Adverb Clauses

Subordinate clauses that function exactly like adverbs by modifying the main clause's verb phrase are called Adverb Clauses. They provide situational details like time, location, cause, purpose, or conditional limits.

Types of Adverb Clauses and Subordinating Conjunctions

Adverb Clause of Time

Conjunctions: after, before, when, while, as soon as, since, till.

Example: Make hay while the sun shines.

Adverb Clause of Reason/Cause

Conjunctions: because, as, since.

Example: The day is warm because the sun is shining.

Adverb Clause of Condition

Conjunctions: if, unless, in case.

Example: If you study hard, you will be successful.

Adverb Clause of Purpose

Conjunctions: so that, in order that.

Example: I wrote it down so that I wouldn't forget.

Adverb Clause of Concession

Conjunctions: though, although, even if.

Example: They studied hard although they were tired.

Adverb Clause of Comparison

Conjunctions: than, as...as.

Example: They answered better than I had hoped.
