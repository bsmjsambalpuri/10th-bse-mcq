Chapter 5: Prepositions

Prepositions are a specific set of words or phrases that establish situational, spatial, or temporal relationships between a noun or pronoun phrase and other words inside a sentence.

1. Categories by Structural Form

  • Single Word: about, across, at, before, beside, during, into, on, through, with.
  • Multi-Word / Phrasal: according to, along with, in front of, in spite of, by means of.
  • Participle Prepositions: barring, concerning, considering, including, regarding.

2. Core Prepositions of Place and Time

Preposition Usage Patterns for Place Usage Patterns for Time
at Specific points or small locations (e.g., at the door, at the station). Exact clock times or holiday periods (e.g., at 5 o'clock, at Christmas).
in Enclosed structures, countries, big cities (e.g., in a room, in Odisha). Months, seasons, years, long blocks of time (e.g., in June, in 2026).
on Surface positions or lines of travel (e.g., on the table, on the river). Specific calendar dates and days (e.g., on Sunday, on 15th August).

3. Noun + Preposition Collocations

Standard Noun-Preposition Combinations:
  • for: Admiration for, Cause for, Demand for, Sympathy for.
  • in: Belief in, Difficulty in, Success in.
  • of: Advantage of, Cause of, Knowledge of, Proof of.
  • to: Access to, Alternative to, Consent to, Solution to.
  • with: Agreement with, Difficulty with, Familiarity with.
