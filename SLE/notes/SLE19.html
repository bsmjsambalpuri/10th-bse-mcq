Chapter 4: Non-finite Verb Forms

A simple sentence must contain a finite verb that changes based on tense and agrees with its subject. Any subsequent verbs inside that same verb phrase are non-finite verbs. They have no direct agreement with the person or number of the subject.

The Four Major Types of Non-Finite Verbs

Type Format Grammatical Name Structural Example
to + verb to-infinitive Seema wants to write the letter.
only the verb Bare infinitive ($\phi$-infinitive) They made Seema write the letter.
verb + -ing Present Participle / Gerund We saw Seema writing the letter.
verb + -ed/-en Past Participle The letters written on the table were typed.

Key Structural Rules

  • Bare Infinitives: Used following causative and perception verbs such as make, let, see, hear, watch, feel (e.g., "I saw him climb the ladder").
  • Verbs of Change: A bare infinitive in an active voice sentence upgrades to a to-infinitive when transformed into the passive voice (e.g., Active: "They made him stand." → Passive: "He was made to stand").

Quick Revision Check

Identify the non-finite verb form: "They must have finished doing their homework."
