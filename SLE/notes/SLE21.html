Chapter 6: Phrasal Verbs

A Phrasal Verb is formed by combining a core standard verb with an adverb particle. This combination creates an idiomatic unit with a completely new meaning distinct from the standalone parts.

1. Structural Classifications

  • Intransitive Phrasal Verbs: Do not take an objective complement. They cannot be turned into the passive voice. (e.g., "The car broke down on the way").
  • Transitive Phrasal Verbs: Require a direct grammatical object. Can be safely passivized. (e.g., "The police put down the violence" → "The violence was put down").

2. High-Yield Board Exam Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal Verb Core Definition Usage Context Example
Bring out To publish / release He has brought out a new grammar book.
Break out To spread out suddenly Cholera broke out everywhere in the village.
Make out To understand clearly I could not make out what he was saying.
Turn down To reject / refuse Why did he turn down your request?
Pass away To die peacefully The old man passed away in his sleep last night.
Run out of To exhaust supplies We have run out of sugar. Please buy some.
