Chapter 1: Tense Revisited

In English Grammar, Time is a continuous flow measured in seconds, minutes, hours, or years, while Tense is a specific grammatical concept. The English language has only two defined tenses: Present and Past. Future time is expressed using various verb forms and modal auxiliaries.

1. Expressing Present Time

A. Present Simple Form

  • Habitual Action: I go to school everyday.
  • Laws of Nature / Scientific Truths: Water boils at 100°C.
  • Permanent State / Fact: Bhubaneswar is the capital of Odisha.

B. Present Progressive Form

  • Happening Now: It is raining (now).
  • Temporary Action: He usually goes on a bicycle, but today he is going on foot.

2. Expressing Past Time

Tense Form Usage Rule Example
Past Simple Action completed at a specific moment or duration in the past. India became free in 1947.
Present Perfect Past action carrying present relevance. I have already done my homework.
Past Perfect Refers to an older action out of two distinct past events. When we reached the station, the train had left.

3. Expressing Future Time

  • Unplanned / Modal Future: Wait a minute. I will come with you.
  • Present Cause / Indication: There are dark clouds in the sky. It is going to rain.
  • Official Program: The match begins at 4:30 PM tomorrow.
